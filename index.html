Short-term preparation

Invest some time

  • Specific wishes? Prepare a lot
  • Start early
  • Know people in the industry
  • Know people to prepare with
  • (Learn what a prepared candidate looks like)

Understand the interviewer's objective

  • Do you have the (analytical) skills of the job?
  • Are you a colleague they would like to work with?
  • Are you a risky hire?

Understand the interview format

(it reflects the interviewer's goals)
  1. Self-introduction, note-taking
  2. Rapport building
  3. Case solving is a surprisingly good proxy
  4. Questions at the end? Not as important IMHO

Case solving

Structuring

  • Do you have the intuitive understanding necessary?
  • Recursively break a problem down
  • Ideally not too "simple", even if the problem is
  • What is the core mechanism of the case?
  • Presenting the structure

Time management

  • The interview, not just the case
  • Usually, the interviewer leads
  • Keep an eye on the scope, not the time

Closing a case

  • Take your time to prepare a statement
  • Pyramid principle, be obvious about it
  • Practice giving 1-minute speeches
